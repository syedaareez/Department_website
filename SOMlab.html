STRENGTH OF MATERIALS LABORATORY

[0 0 2 1]

Course Objectives

These objectives are formulated to demonstrate the basic principles in the area of strength and mechanics of materials to the undergraduate students through a series of experiments. The CO is given below:

  1. Students will be able to determine various mechanical properties of material like Youngâ€™s modulus, tensile strength and percentage elongation for steel, aluminium, brass and cast iron specimens on universal testing machine and shall be able to plot the stress strain diagram.

  2. Students will be able to perform the compression test for cast iron specimen on universal testing machine.

  3. Students will be able to measure the total energy absorbed during sudden loading of the ductile specimen on Charpy and Izod setup. So as to recommend the material for various applications.

  4. Students will be able to determine the stiffness of the cantilever beam and Spring under compressive and tensile loading and analyze the loading patterns.

  5. Students will be able to plot and study the S-N curve for steel, aluminium and fiber reinforced composite material at 25%, 50%, 60% and 75% of ultimate tensile strength of the specimen.

  6. Students will be able to determine the deflection for mild steel specimen and verify the beam formula for specimen in bending.

  7. Students will be able determine Brinell hardness and Rockwell hardness on various specimen and recommend the appropriate material for various application.

  8. Students will be able to analyse the behaviour of steel and aluminium specimen under torsion.

Course Outcomes
  1. Students will be able to determine Young’s modulus, tensile strength and percentage elongation for steel, aluminium, brass and cast iron specimens on universal testing machine and shall be able to plot the stress strain diagram.

  2. Students will be able to perform the compression test for cast iron specimen on universal testing machine.

  3. Students will be able to determine the deflection for mild steel specimen and verify the beam formula for specimen in bending.

  4. Students will be able to determine the stiffness of the following: (i) Cantilever beam. (ii)  Spring under compressive and tensile loading.

  5. Students will be able to measure the total energy absorbed in fracture of the ductile specimen on Charpy and Izod setup.

  6. Students will be able to plot and study the S-N curve for steel, aluminium and fiber reinforced composite material at 25%, 50%, 60% and 75% of ultimate tensile strength of the specimen.

  7. Students will be able to prepare specimen for hardness test.

  8. Students will be able to perform tests on prepared specimens for Brinell hardness and Rockwell hardness.

  9. Students will be able to study the behavior of steel and aluminium specimen under torsion.

List of Practicals
  1. Determination of Young's modulus, tensile, strength and percentage elongation for steel, aluminum, brass and cast iron specimens on universal testing machine. Also plot the stress strain diagram.

  2. To perform the compression test for cast iron specimen on universal testing machine.

  3. To determine the deflection for mild steel specimen and verify the beam formula for specimen in bending.

  4. To determine the stiffness of the following: (i) Cantilever beam (ii) Spring under compressive and tensile loading

  5. To measure the total energy absorbed in fracturing of the ductile specimen on Charpy and Izod setup.

  6. To plot and study the S-N curve for steel, aluminum and fibre reinforced composite material at 25%, 50%, 60% and 75% of ultimate tensile strength of the specimen.

  7. Preparation of specimen for hardness test.

  8. Testing of prepared specimens for Brinell hardness and Rockwell hardness.

  9. To study the behavior of steel and aluminum specimen under torsion.
